Who is Christ?


Jesus Christ, also known as the Son of God, is a central figure in Christianity. He is believed to be the savior of humanity and is revered for his life, teachings, and sacrifice.


His teachings emphasized love, forgiveness, and compassion. His messages are recorded in the New Testament of the Bible, and they continue to influence the lives of billions of people worldwide.


According to the Gospels, Jesus performed various miracles, such as healing the sick, turning water into wine, and feeding the hungry. These acts are seen as signs of his divinity.


He was crucified on a cross as a means of execution. This event, known as the Crucifixion, is central to Christian belief. It is believed that his sacrifice atoned for the sins of humanity.
